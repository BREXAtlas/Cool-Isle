Cooling on a budget

Keep your cool without melting your bank balance

Tried-and-tested tricks for British homes — most cost pennies. Each hack links to the kit that makes it work harder.

1. Curtains shut by day, windows open by night

Close curtains and blinds on sun-facing windows before 9am. After sunset, open windows on opposite sides of the home for a through-draught.

Cost: free

2. The wet-sheet trick

Hang a damp sheet in front of an open window or a fan — evaporative cooling, Victorian style. Pairs brilliantly with a tower fan.

Cost: free–£90

3. Bowl of ice in front of a fan

The classic. A frozen water bottle works even better and refreezes overnight. Upgrade: an evaporative cooler with ice packs does this properly.

Cost: pennies

4. Seal that AC window gap

A portable AC with an unsealed window is cooling the street. A window seal kit can noticeably improve cooling and cut running costs.

Cost: ~£25, saves on every hour of use

5. Switch off heat-makers

Ovens, tumble dryers, gaming PCs and even chargers pump heat into your rooms. Cook cold meals, dry laundry outside, and unplug what you're not using.

Cost: free — and cuts your bill

6. Cotton sheets, hot water bottle... frozen

Swap to cotton or linen bedding, and fill a hot water bottle with cold water and freeze it for a bed cooler. Genuinely works.

Cost: free–£12

7. Cool the person, not the room

Cold flannel on wrists and neck, feet in a basin of cool water, a handheld fan for the commute. Cooling your body directly uses a fraction of the energy.

Cost: pennies

8. Check on neighbours

Heat is most dangerous for older people and those with health conditions. Knock on for elderly neighbours during amber alerts — it's the British thing to do. NHS heat advice: nhs.uk.

Cost: free, value: priceless

The 20-year picture

Britain is heating up — the trend, not the blip

Hottest recorded UK temperature by notable year. Two decades ago, 35°C made headlines. In 2022 the UK crossed 40°C for the first time in recorded history, and recent summers keep pushing amber and red alerts. The Met Office's UK climate projections point to hotter, drier summers and warmer, wetter winters becoming the norm.

How does the UK compare to the neighbours?

Record maximum temperatures. Our neighbours run hotter — but their homes are built for it. Only a small minority of UK homes have any air conditioning, which is why British heatwaves hit harder at lower temperatures.

Climate responsibility

Cool the home, not the planet

Every product we recommend is chosen with running cost and carbon in mind: fans before coolers, coolers before compressors, and compressors sized right so they don't waste energy. Passive measures first — shading, sealing, insulation — then efficient kit. That's cheaper for you and lighter on the grid.

Which BTU do I need?

Rule of thumb for UK rooms: ~350 BTU per square metre. South-facing rooms and lofts need 15–25% more.

About CoolIsle UK

Sustainable heat and cooling for every British home

CoolIsle exists because Britain's weather has changed faster than Britain's homes. Our houses — from Victorian terraces to Glasgow tenements — were masterpieces of their era, built to hold warmth through long grey winters. Nobody designed them for 40°C. Our goal is simple: help every household stay safe and comfortable in heatwaves, cold snaps and downpours alike, with solutions that respect both your budget and the environment.

We take our cue from a very British tradition of environmental stewardship — one championed for over five decades by His Majesty King Charles III, who spoke about sustainability long before it was fashionable. In that spirit, we always recommend the lowest-energy option that solves your problem: shade before fans, fans before coolers, coolers before compressors, and insulation before everything. Frequently asked questions

Do portable air conditioners work in UK homes?

Yes — a 9,000 BTU unit handles a typical double bedroom (12–18m²), needs no installation and no landlord permission. Window kits fit standard UK casement and sliding windows.

How much does a portable AC cost to run in the UK?

Roughly 20–32p per hour at current electricity rates. Evaporative coolers run at 3–6p/hr and fans just 1–3p/hr — which is why we suggest the cheapest option that actually solves your problem.

Does the UK really have a cold problem too?

A serious one. Britain's housing stock is among the least insulated in western Europe, and cold homes are linked to thousands of excess winter deaths each year. Draught-proofing and insulation are the highest-impact fixes — see our Winter Ready section and the NHS Keep Warm Keep Well guidance.

Air conditioner vs air cooler — what's the difference?

An air conditioner uses a compressor to genuinely lower room temperature and remove humidity. An evaporative cooler blows air over water — far cheaper to run, but in muggy British heat it can make humid air feel worse. For sticky 30°C+ nights, choose a compressor AC.

Can I use a US air conditioner in the UK?

No — US units run on 115V/60Hz; UK mains is 230V/50Hz. Buy UK-spec units with a UK plug and UKCA marking.

Where does the live temperature data come from?

The Open-Meteo public weather API. It's indicative only — for official warnings always check the Met Office and UKHSA heat-health alerts, both linked on this page.
