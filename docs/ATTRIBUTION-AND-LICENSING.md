# Attribution and licensing

## Required product notices

Place these near relevant WeatherChart data views and in the sources page:

> Powered by Met Office data — data supplied by the Met Office.

> WeatherChart UK is an independent weather-information companion. It is not affiliated with or endorsed by the Met Office.

> Always follow current official Met Office warnings and emergency guidance.

Where a source is genuinely supplied under the Open Government Licence v3.0, use:

> Contains public sector information licensed under the Open Government Licence v3.0.

Do not apply that OGL statement to Global Spot merely because the Met Office is a public body. Global Spot is governed by the product-specific Weather DataHub terms recorded in `MET-OFFICE-SOURCE-AUDIT.md`.

## Met Office

- Retain direct RSS links and source titles/dates.
- Display Met Office attribution beside warnings and derived forecast visualisations.
- Do not use the Met Office logo unless separate brand permission and current guidelines are confirmed.
- Do not imply that WeatherChart or Cool Isle is official, sponsored, approved, or endorsed.
- Do not copy article bodies, substantial passages, website imagery, or page design.
- Do not insert an intermediate redirect between an RSS card and its Met Office destination.

## Open-Meteo

Label any Open-Meteo result “Open-Meteo indicative fallback” and link to https://open-meteo.com/. Never present it as an official Met Office forecast or warning.

## Maps

Display “© OpenStreetMap contributors” on every OpenStreetMap-backed map and preserve the tile provider’s required attribution. Leaflet is used under its published open-source licence; keep the library version and source recorded in the deployment output.

## Social platforms

Public post metadata remains owned by the author/platform and is displayed only as permitted by the configured API or embed terms. Cards link to the original post, expire promptly, and support correction/removal. Platform names are source labels, not endorsements.

## Images

No Met Office image is used. Any future photograph must be downloaded only when its licence permits repository redistribution, optimised locally, given descriptive alternative text, and recorded in `IMAGE-CREDITS.md`.
