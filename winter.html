The other half of being weather-ready

❄️ Winter-Ready: keep the warmth you've paid for

Britain's real weather emergency isn't the heatwave — it's the freeze. Our homes are among the draughtiest in western Europe, and cold homes are linked to thousands of excess winter deaths each year. The good news: most of the fix costs less than one month's heating bill.

Why it matters

The cold, hard picture

18°CThe minimum indoor temperature health experts recommend — many UK homes struggle to hold it.
Thousandsof excess winter deaths in the UK each year are linked to cold homes and circulating illness.
Millionsof UK households are in fuel poverty — forced to choose between heating and other essentials.
Pre-1930sA huge share of British housing predates modern insulation standards entirely.

These figures are indicative — for current statistics see the Office for National Statistics and GOV.UK fuel poverty statistics.

The plan

Eight steps to a warmer home — cheapest first

The golden rule: heat the person, then the room, then the house. Work down this list and stop when you're warm.

Draught-proof doors, letterbox and keyholes

The single highest-impact cheap fix. Brush strips, letterbox flaps and a classic sausage-dog draught excluder.

Cost: £5–£20 · pays for itself in weeks

Thermal curtain liners — closed at dusk

Windows lose heat fastest. Liners hook onto your existing curtains; close them the moment the light fades.

Cost: ~£35 a pair · bonus: doubles as summer blackout shade

Window insulation film (renters' secret weapon)

Temporary secondary glazing: clear film, tape, hairdryer. Fully removable — landlord-proof.

Cost: ~£13 per multi-window kit

Heated throw over the sofa, not the thermostat up

A low-energy heated throw warms you for pennies per evening versus pounds for whole-house heating.

Cost: ~£50 once · pennies per evening

Bleed radiators & clear the space in front of them

Cold at the top means trapped air. A radiator key costs pence. And a sofa parked against a radiator is heating the sofa.

Cost: under £1

Reverse your ceiling fan (yes, really)

Most fans have a winter switch — clockwise on low pushes risen warm air back down. The summer fan earns its keep twice.

Cost: free

Manage condensation before it becomes damp

Colder, better-sealed homes can grow condensation. Ventilate briefly after showers and cooking; a small dehumidifier protects walls and lungs alike.

Cost: free–£120

Check what support you're owed

Winter Fuel Payment, Cold Weather Payment, Warm Home Discount, supplier hardship funds — millions go unclaimed every year. Links below.

Cost: free · worth up to hundreds
