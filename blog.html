What size portable air conditioner do I need? (UK room chart)

Buy too small and it runs flat-out without ever cooling the room. Buy too big and you've wasted money and energy. The UK rule of thumb is simple: about 350 BTU per square metre, plus 15–25% for south-facing rooms, big windows or loft conversions.

RoomTypical sizeBTU class
Box room / home officeup to 12m²5,000–7,000 BTU
Double bedroom12–18m²8,000–9,000 BTU
Living room18–28m²10,000–12,000 BTU
Open-plan kitchen/lounge28–40m²14,000–16,000 BTU

Measure your room (length × width in metres), multiply by 350, and round up to the nearest class. South-facing with a bay window? Add 20%.

Portable air conditioner running costs UK 2026 — real numbers

The honest answer most retailers won't give you: a typical 9,000 BTU portable AC draws about 1.0–1.1 kW, which at current UK electricity prices works out to roughly 20–32p per hour.

  • 4 hours a day through a 60-day summer: around £50–£75 for the season.
  • Evaporative air cooler: 3–6p per hour — a fraction of the cost, but it cools you, not the whole room.
  • Tower or pedestal fan: 1–3p per hour. Running one all night costs less than a biscuit.

Three ways to cut AC costs without suffering: seal the window kit properly (an unsealed window can waste a large share of the cooling), pre-cool the room in the evening rather than fighting peak heat at 3pm, and close the door — a portable AC cools one room, not a house.

Rule of the house: cool the smallest room you actually live in, not the biggest room you own.

Air cooler vs air conditioner: which works in humid British heat?

They look similar; they are not the same machine.

An air conditioner has a compressor and refrigerant. It genuinely lowers the temperature and pulls moisture out of the air — which matters, because British heatwaves are often muggy. That dehumidifying effect is half the relief.

An evaporative air cooler blows air across water. It creates a lovely cool breeze in dry heat, costs pennies to run, and needs no window hose. But in humid heat it adds moisture to already-muggy air, which can make a sticky night feel stickier.

  • Sticky 30°C+ nights, need to sleep: compressor AC.
  • Dry warm days, desk or sofa relief, tight budget: evaporative cooler.
  • Any budget, any weather, moving air feels 3–4° cooler: a decent fan — never underestimate one.

How to fit a portable AC hose to a UK casement window

British casement windows (the side-hinged kind) confuse every AC manual written for American sliding sashes. Here's the five-minute method:

  • 1. Open the casement to its first latch position.
  • 2. Fit a fabric window seal kit around the frame and the opened pane — the zip tape sticks to the frame, the fabric wraps the window.
  • 3. Zip the seal closed, leaving just the hose outlet open.
  • 4. Push the exhaust hose through the outlet and zip snug around it.
  • 5. Keep the hose as short and straight as possible — every bend costs you cooling.

No drilling, no permanent fixings, completely renter-friendly — and your landlord will never know.
